How to read this map

Modes

Margins: colors show the Rep − Dem margin (as % of total votes). Darker = larger absolute margin.

Winners: solid color by winner (D/R), regardless of margin size.

Shift: change in signed margin vs the nearest available prior election for that contest (e.g. 2020->2024). Positive = more Republican; negative = more Democratic.

Margin categories

Category names are shorthand for absolute margin ranges:

  • Annihilation: ≥40%
  • Dominant: 30-39.99%
  • Stronghold: 20-29.99%
  • Safe: 10-19.99%
  • Likely: 5.50-9.99%
  • Lean: 1.00-5.49%
  • Tilt: 0.50-0.99%
  • Tossup: <0.50%

Example: "Safe Republican (10-19.99%)" means the Republican candidate leads by 10-19.99 percentage points (as % of total votes).

Hover + Pin

  • Hover a shape to see a quick result card.
  • Click Pin in the tooltip to freeze it (so it won't change while you move the mouse).
  • Use Close to dismiss a pinned tooltip.

Controls

  • P toggles precinct overlay (dots at statewide zoom; polygons when zoomed in).
  • L toggles county labels.
  • Colorblind toggles colorblind-friendly colors.
  • Clear (in the vote counter) unpins a selected area.

Notes

If Shift can't find a prior year for the selected contest, it falls back to Margins.
